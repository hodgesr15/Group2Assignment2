Ridge Hodges's Biography

Early Life

He was born in Greenville, NC on March 21, 1993 at Pitt Memorial hospital. At a very young age he was inspired by playing sports such as: basketball, football, soccer, and baseball. He was raised in Washington, NC from birth up until 4th grade by his aunt and grandma. He then moved to Rocky Mount, NC to live with his father who worked for Crop Production Services. Ridge resided there with his father from 4th grade up until 9th grade where he was involved in all sports recreationally. During these years he enjoyed riding bikes with friends and going fishing with his brother.

High School

He attended Nash Central High School his freshman year in 2008 in which he didnt play any sports. He soon after that transferred to Washington High School in Washington, NC to live with his aunt and grandmother. It was there that he played on the JV basketball team his sophomore year and was the leading scorer. The following 2 years at the high school he was named team captain and a starter on the Varsity squad. He was recruited by multiple colleges along the east coast, but decided to attend Pitt Community College on an basketball scholarship.

Collegiate Career

He initially attended Pitt Community College on an basketball scholarship where he studied General Education classes to prepare to transfer. Both years on the basketball team he didn't start, but was an intricate part of the bench. Ridge was named an academic all american during his tenure at Pitt Community College. He soon transferred to East Carolina University to major in Information and Computer Technology. He currently works in the ITCS department working with the classroom technology team handling any computer tickets in the classrooms on ECU's campus.

Military Career

Ridge enlisted into the Air Force Reserves in September 2013. He attended military boot camp at Lackland Air Force Base in San Antonio, Texas for 2 months. Immeaditely following basic military training he trasnferred to Good Fellow Air Force Base to attend Technical training school to learn how to be an intelligence analyst for 5 months. After that tour he officially transferred to his permanent duty station which is Langley Air Force Base in Hampton, Virginia. He is currently still serving at the base location as an traditional reservist and is looking to serve for at least 20 years.
